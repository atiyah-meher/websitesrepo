Get It Done: Explore the psychology behind motivation and achieving goals

-Ayelet Fishbach

Seven lessons from "Get It Done: Explore the psychology behind motivation and achieving goals"

  1. Choose the right goals: Not all goals are created equal. Fishbach emphasizes setting specific, challenging, yet achievable goals that align with your values and interests. This increases your motivation and commitment to achieving them.

  2. Conquer the "middle problem": The initial excitement of a new goal often fades, leaving you stuck in the middle. Fishbach suggests strategies like progress tracking, micro-goals, and public commitment to stay motivated during this crucial stage.

  3. Tame your temptations: Distractions and procrastination are major hurdles. Fishbach recommends techniques like pre-commitment, timeboxing, and reward systems to help you stay focused on your goals and resist temptations.

  4. Leverage the power of social influence: We're social creatures, and others can significantly impact our motivation. Fishbach encourages finding accountability partners, sharing goals with supportive people, and celebrating achievements to boost your progress.

  5. Reframe failure as learning: Mistakes and setbacks are inevitable. Fishbach suggests viewing them as opportunities for learning and growth rather than personal failures. This positive mindset can fuel resilience and future success.

  6. Harness the power of small wins: Celebrating small victories along the way keeps you motivated. Fishbach recommends acknowledging and appreciating your progress, no matter how small, to maintain momentum and avoid feeling overwhelmed.

  7. Use implementation intentions: "If-then" statements like "if I have free time, then I will work on my project" can significantly increase your follow-through. Fishbach suggests using these implementation intentions to automate your behavior and make progress even when you're not feeling motivated.
